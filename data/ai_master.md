| 2020 The Year That Set the Stage |
| :---: |

## **2020 Overview**

2020 was the quiet explosion. The AI world was still dominated by academia and well-funded labs — but three events changed the trajectory permanently: GPT-3 showed the world that language models could scale to jaw-dropping capability, AlphaFold solved a 50-year biological mystery, and the COVID-19 pandemic turbo-charged digital adoption globally, creating massive demand for automation and AI tools that would fuel investment for years to come.

### **Key Stats: 2020**

| 2020 BY THE NUMBERS Roughly 20% of global businesses reported using AI in at least one function • Global AI startup funding: \~$36B • GPT-3 launched with 175B parameters • AlphaFold wins CASP14 with near-perfect protein structure prediction • OpenAI valued at \~$1B |
| :---- |

### **2020 Event Timeline**

| Jan 2020 | Google Research releases Meena, a 2.6B parameter conversational AI model that hinted at more naturalistic multi-turn dialogue. Sets the stage for the chatbot arms race. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Jan 2020** | OpenAI GPT-2 (1.5B params, released in stages through late 2019\) fully released to the public. OpenAI originally withheld it citing 'misuse concerns' — a policy debate that foreshadows years of safety vs. openness tension. |
| **Feb 2020** | Scale AI raises $100M Series C, valuing the data labeling startup at $1B. Data quality for AI becomes a serious business. |
| **Jun 2020** | OpenAI releases GPT-3 via API (175 billion parameters). Trained on \~570GB of filtered internet text. Demonstrates few-shot and zero-shot learning. Developers immediately begin building products on the API. Described as 'the most powerful language model ever built.' |
| **Jun 2020** | Microsoft acquires exclusive license to GPT-3. Partnership deepens and sets up the $1B+ investment chain to come. |
| **Jul 2020** | OpenAI releases DALL-E 1 in concept form (image generation from text). Publicly revealed January 2021 but developed in 2020\. |
| **Jul 2020** | GitHub introduces Codex research project internally (public launch 2021). AI-assisted coding begins in earnest. |
| **Sep 2020** | Anthropic co-founders still at OpenAI — Dario Amodei is VP of Research, sister Daniela is VP of Operations. Internal tensions about safety and commercialization are building. |
| **Oct 2020** | Google Research introduces Vision Transformer (ViT), applying the Transformer architecture to image recognition. Major shift from CNN-based models. |
| **Nov 2020** | DeepMind's AlphaFold wins CASP14 (Critical Assessment of protein Structure Prediction) with stunning accuracy, effectively solving the 50-year-old 'protein folding problem.' Nobel Prize implications. Instantly recognized as one of the greatest AI achievements in history. |
| **Nov 2020** | NeurIPS 2020 showcases explosion of ML research papers. Transformer architecture dominates most fields. |
| **Dec 2020** | GPT-3 has generated enormous excitement. Over 300 apps built on the API in its first few months. First signs of the 'API economy' for AI. |

### **2020 Company Landscape**

* OpenAI — Dominant lab, GPT-3 is THE model. Nonprofit/for-profit hybrid structure.

* DeepMind (Google) — AlphaFold triumph; massive research output but no public consumer product.

* Google AI / Google Brain — BERT, T5, ViT research; building toward LaMDA.

* Facebook AI Research (FAIR) — Publishing research but no breakout product moment.

* Hugging Face — Emerging as the GitHub of AI models; Transformers library released 2019 gains massive traction.

* Scale AI — Quietly becoming the backbone of AI data labeling across the industry.

* OpenAI competitors were sparse — the field was not yet crowded at the frontier.

| 2021 Language Models Grow Up |
| :---: |

## **2021 Overview**

2021 saw AI move from research papers to real products. GitHub Copilot democratized AI coding. DALL-E introduced image generation to the mainstream. Google's LaMDA sparked the first major AI sentience debate. Most importantly, a landmark split at OpenAI led to the founding of Anthropic — setting up the most consequential rivalry in AI history. The VC money started flowing seriously, and the 'AI startup' became a fundable category.

### **2021 Event Timeline**

| Jan 2021 | OpenAI publicly releases DALL-E (1st gen): a 12B parameter transformer model that generates images from text prompts. Goes viral. 'A corgi wearing a tutu eating a cupcake' becomes the demo that captivates the internet. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Jan 2021** | OpenAI releases CLIP (Contrastive Language-Image Pre-Training): bridges NLP and computer vision. Becomes the backbone for future image AI systems including Stable Diffusion. |
| **Feb 2021** | Hugging Face raises $40M Series B. Their open model hub is becoming essential infrastructure for AI researchers and companies worldwide. |
| **Mar 2021** | Tesla Autopilot and FSD (Full Self-Driving) debates intensify. Elon Musk's AI promises vs. regulatory reality become a major public discourse. |
| **May 2021** | Google Research presents LaMDA (Language Model for Dialogue Applications) at Google I/O. 137B parameter conversational model. Sets stage for both Bard and the Blake Lemoine 'sentience' controversy in 2022\. |
| **Jun 2021** | GitHub Copilot (powered by OpenAI Codex) enters technical preview. Developers can now have AI write code in real-time in VS Code. Perhaps the most impactful AI product launch of the year for developers. |
| **Jun 2021** | OpenAI raises $125M additional funding. Value approaches $3B. Microsoft relationship deepens. |
| **Jul 2021** | DeepMind releases AlphaFold 2 in full, plus opens the AlphaFold Protein Structure Database with 350,000 structures. Later expanded to 200 million proteins (nearly all known proteins). Called 'the most significant contribution AI has made to science.' |
| **Aug 2021** | Dario Amodei, Daniela Amodei, and \~9 others resign from OpenAI citing disagreements over safety culture and commercialization speed. They found Anthropic. This is the most consequential talent departure in AI history. |
| **Sep 2021** | Anthropic incorporated with \~$124M in seed funding from a group including Google. The 'Constitutional AI' safety philosophy begins development internally. |
| **Sep 2021** | NVidia acquires Mellanox (2020) has already closed; now announces plans to acquire Arm for $40B — later blocked by regulators. Nvidia's AI chip ambitions become clear. |
| **Oct 2021** | Microsoft and OpenAI deepen partnership. Azure becomes the exclusive cloud for OpenAI training runs. |
| **Nov 2021** | Stable Diffusion's core research (latent diffusion models) published by Runway ML and LMU Munich. Lays groundwork for the 2022 image AI explosion. |
| **Dec 2021** | AI-generated art starts appearing in online communities. First glimmers of the creator economy disruption to come. |

| Anthropic Founded Aug 2021 | Founded by Dario Amodei, Daniela Amodei, Tom Brown, Chris Olah, Sam McCandlish, Jack Clark, and Jared Kaplan — all from OpenAI. Mission: AI safety research and deployment. Later becomes the \#2 AI lab globally. |

| Cohere Founded 2019, grew 2021 | Enterprise NLP startup. Raised $40M in 2021\. Focuses on helping businesses use LLMs via API. Competes directly with OpenAI's API business. Valued at $2.1B by 2023\. |

| Inflection AI Founded 2021 | Founded by Reid Hoffman and Mustafa Suleyman (DeepMind co-founder). Raised $1.3B in 2023\. Built Pi, a personal AI. Effectively dissolved in 2024 when Microsoft hired Suleyman and most staff. |

| 2022 The Generative AI Explosion |
| :---: |

## **2022 Overview**

2022 was the year generative AI stopped being a research curiosity and became a civilization-shaping technology. Stable Diffusion and DALL-E 2 made image AI free and available to everyone. Midjourney spawned a new creative culture. Then — in November — ChatGPT arrived and broke the internet. Reaching 1 million users in 5 days and 100 million in 2 months, it became the fastest-adopted consumer product in history. Meanwhile, major tech companies began panicking, Google declared 'Code Red,' and the AI investment floodgates opened.

### **2022 Event Timeline**

| Jan 2022 | Google Brain and DeepMind announce merger discussions (completed 2023). Google's AI organizational structure begins major consolidation. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Feb 2022** | Stable Diffusion research goes public. Runway ML, Stability AI, and academic collaborators publish latent diffusion models paper. |
| **Apr 2022** | OpenAI releases DALL-E 2 — dramatically more powerful than DALL-E 1\. Photorealistic images from text prompts. Joins waitlist of 1 million+ within days. |
| **Apr 2022** | Google releases PaLM (Pathways Language Model) — 540B parameters. Shows 'emergent abilities' at scale including multi-step reasoning. 'Few-shot' performance rivals GPT-3. |
| **May 2022** | Google engineer Blake Lemoine claims LaMDA is sentient in a viral memo. Google places him on administrative leave and eventually fires him. Triggers first mainstream debate about AI consciousness. |
| **Jul 2022** | Midjourney launches in open beta via Discord. Within months, Midjourney-generated art wins competitions and goes viral. Creates massive cultural and legal controversy around AI art. |
| **Jul 2022** | Stability AI raises $101M at a $1B valuation. Emad Mostaque becomes a prominent face of the open-source AI movement. |
| **Aug 2022** | Stability AI releases Stable Diffusion 1.0 — fully open source, weights downloadable, runs on consumer GPUs. This is a watershed moment: democratizes AI image generation completely. The floodgates open. |
| **Sep 2022** | DALL-E 2 opens to all users. Over 1.5 million daily image generations within weeks. |
| **Sep 2022** | Adobe acquires Figma for $20B — AI-powered design becomes a major strategic focus across creative tools. |

| **Nov 2022** | OpenAI releases ChatGPT as a 'research preview' on November 30\. Powered by GPT-3.5 with RLHF fine-tuning. Within 5 days: 1M users. Within 2 months: 100M users. Fastest consumer app in history. Tech, media, education, government — all in shock. |
| **Nov 2022** | Google internally declares 'Code Red' in response to ChatGPT. CEO Sundar Pichai pulls Google co-founders Larry Page and Sergey Brin out of retirement to advise. Emergency acceleration of Bard begins. |
| **Dec 2022** | Anthropic releases Claude 1 (internal/limited). Focused on safety via Constitutional AI. Begins conversations with Google about investment. |
| **Dec 2022** | Tech layoffs begin in earnest at big companies: Meta (11K), Amazon (10K), Twitter (50% of workforce), Lyft, Stripe, and hundreds of startups cut staff amid the interest rate hike cycle. |

### **2022 Investment Landscape**

| 2022 FUNDING SNAPSHOT Global AI startup funding: \~$45.8B for the year, up dramatically from prior years. Late 2022 saw the beginning of the broader VC funding crunch, but AI was about to become the one sector that kept attracting capital. Stability AI: $101M. Midjourney: bootstrapped, profitable almost immediately. Cohere: raised $125M Series C at $2.1B valuation. OpenAI: cumulative investment from Microsoft reaches $3B+ range. |

### **2022: The Rise of Open Source AI**

* Stable Diffusion releases: open weights \= anyone can run locally, fine-tune, modify. The 'hugging face' ecosystem explodes.

* LAION-5B dataset released: 5 billion image-text pairs used to train image models. Open data for open AI.

* EleutherAI community publishes GPT-NeoX-20B. First major open-source LLM at GPT-3 scale.

* Meta releases OPT-175B (Open Pre-trained Transformers) — their GPT-3 equivalent, open weights. Controversial: leaked immediately.

| 2023 The Year AI Ate the World |
| :---: |

## **2023 Overview**

If 2022 ended with ChatGPT's explosion, 2023 was the year the rest of the world scrambled to catch up, cash in, or regulate. GPT-4 arrived and shocked researchers. Google raced to launch Bard (and fumbled the demo). Meta released Llama and triggered the open-source revolution. Anthropic launched Claude publicly. Microsoft put Copilot in every Office product. Investment poured in at record pace despite the broader VC winter. And then in November — the most dramatic corporate governance crisis in tech history: OpenAI fired and rehired Sam Altman in five chaotic days, threatening to take down the company that had just changed civilization.

### **The OpenAI Coup — Deep Analysis**

| THE 5-DAY CRISIS: NOV 17-22, 2023 The OpenAI boardroom drama was the most dramatic event in AI history outside of actual technical breakthroughs. Root cause: structural tension between OpenAI's non-profit mission and its $29B valuation for-profit arm. The board (Helen Toner, Ilya Sutskever, Adam D'Angelo, Tasha McCauley) had legal authority to fire the CEO with no investor input. They cited 'not being consistently candid' — later reporting linked it to safety disagreements and allegations of manipulative behavior. Microsoft's $13B investment gave it zero board seats. The chaos forced a near-death experience that ultimately reinforced Altman's power: the new board is composed of establishment business figures with no safety ideologues. Ilya Sutskever later left OpenAI to found SSI (Safe Superintelligence). Ilya publicly expressed regret for participating in the firing. |

| 2024 The Race Becomes a War |
| :---: |

## **2024 Overview**

2024 was the year AI became unavoidable infrastructure. The model race accelerated to a dizzying pace: GPT-4o brought multimodal AI to everyone for free, Claude 3 Opus briefly claimed the \#1 reasoning spot, Gemini 1.5 introduced million-token context windows, and Llama 3 made open-source nearly match closed frontier models. Reasoning models arrived (o1). AI agents emerged from prototype to product. Nvidia became the most valuable company on earth. And the geopolitical dimension sharpened — US chip export controls tightened, but China's AI labs kept advancing anyway.

### **2024 Themes Analysis**

Jensen Huang's 'bet the company' on AI data center chips paid off beyond all projections. H100 GPUs sell at 10x MSRP on grey market. Nvidia's CUDA ecosystem becomes the inescapable platform for AI training. AMD, Intel, Google TPUs, and Amazon Trainium all try to compete but Nvidia retains \~80% AI chip market share. |
| :---- |

| THE REASONING MODEL SHIFT OpenAI's o1 introduced 'inference-time compute scaling' — spending more compute at inference to reason step-by-step. This was as significant as scaling training compute. The model doesn't just predict the next token — it thinks through a problem. o3 extends this dramatically. The implication: you can trade money (compute) for intelligence in real-time. This opens a new axis of AI capability beyond model size. |

| 2025 DeepSeek Shock, Agents & the AGI Debate |
| :---: |

## **2025 Overview**

2025 opened with an earthquake: DeepSeek R1 from a Chinese hedge fund's AI lab matched OpenAI's o1 — reportedly trained for under $6M. It triggered what markets called a 'Sputnik moment,' wiping $600B off Nvidia's market cap in a single day. The year then saw the rise of AI agents as a real product category, GPT-5, the Gemini 3 challenge to OpenAI's throne, Claude Code becoming the favorite coding agent, open-source models from China gaining global adoption, and the AI job displacement debate turning from theoretical to undeniably real.

## **The AI Hardware & Semiconductor War**

The AI revolution is a hardware story as much as a software story. Whoever controls the chips controls the capability to train and run AI models. This has become a geopolitical battleground.

### **Nvidia's Domination**

* 2020 — A100 GPU: the first purpose-built AI training chip at massive scale. Becomes the standard for AI data centers.

* 2022 — H100 GPU (Hopper architecture): 6x the AI performance of A100. Immediately backordered. Wait times hit 6-12 months. Grey market prices reach 3x-4x MSRP.

* 2023-24 — Nvidia revenues go from $27B (2023) to $130B (2024). Greatest corporate growth story in history.

* 2024 — H200: further improvement. B100/B200 (Blackwell) announced for 2025 — 30x better inference performance than H100.

* 2024 (Jun) — Nvidia briefly surpasses Apple and Microsoft as most valuable company at \~$3.3T.

* 2025 — Blackwell architecture ships to hyperscalers. Demand exceeds supply. Jensen Huang: 'It will be sold out for a while.'

### **US Export Controls on China**

* Oct 2022 — Biden admin bans export of A100 and H100 GPUs to China. Also bans advanced semiconductor manufacturing equipment.

* Oct 2023 — Controls tightened further. H800 (China-specific downgrade) also restricted. Any chip above performance threshold banned.

* 2024 — Despite controls, Chinese labs like DeepSeek demonstrate frontier performance. They used stockpiled A100s and optimized training algorithms.

* 2025 — DeepSeek shock proves export controls have limits. Chinese labs inventing around chip restrictions through software efficiency.

* 2025 — Ongoing: US considers restricting cloud access to advanced AI from China. Global chip alliance discussions.

### **Competing Chip Players**

* AMD — MI300X AI accelerator competes with H100 on some workloads. AMD acquires ZT Systems, Silo AI, Brium, Untether AI, Enosemi (2024-25) to build full AI stack.

* Google TPUs — Tensor Processing Units (v4, v5) power Google's own AI training and serving. Not available to external users at scale.

* Amazon Trainium / Inferentia — AWS's custom chips for AI training and inference. Used internally and offered to AWS customers.

* Microsoft Azure Maia — Microsoft's custom AI chip. Complement to Nvidia in Azure.

* Apple Silicon (M-series) — Best-in-class local AI inference. M4 chip enables on-device LLM running.

* Intel Gaudi — Competes with Nvidia but gains little traction. Intel struggles in the AI chip era.

* Cerebras — Wafer-scale AI chips for extreme parallel computation. Niche but growing.

* Groq — Blazing fast inference chips. LPU (Language Processing Unit) architecture. 'Fastest LLM inference in the world' claims.

### **Key Business Model Strategies**

### **The API Economy Model (OpenAI, Anthropic)**

Charge developers per-token to call the model via API. OpenAI went from $28M ARR (2022) to $3.7B ARR (2024) primarily through API access. Anthropic's API powers thousands of products. Risk: margin compression as models get cheaper and competition intensifies.

### **The Platform Bet (Microsoft, Google)**

Embed AI into existing products (Office 365 Copilot, Google Workspace Duet AI) that already have billions of users. Microsoft pays OpenAI, Google builds Gemini in-house. Revenue upside through subscription upsells. Azure and Google Cloud also profit from AI compute demand.

### **The Open Source Play (Meta, Mistral)**

Release model weights freely to build ecosystem dominance and reduce rivals' competitive moat. Meta's strategy: Llama being widely adopted means less reason to pay OpenAI or Anthropic. Mistral: European open-source alternative, enterprise API alongside open weights.

### **The Safety Premium (Anthropic)**

Position as the 'responsible' lab. Constitutional AI. Enterprise-first. Attract companies that need compliance-safe AI. Claude's brand emphasizes trustworthiness. Backfire risk: safety constraints can reduce capability in ways users notice.

### **The Vertical AI Startup**

Build a specialized AI product for a specific industry: Harvey (legal AI), Hippocratic AI (healthcare), Glean (enterprise search), Cursor/Windsurf (developer tools). These are 2022-2026's fastest-growing category. Often raise enormous rounds on thin revenue by showing AI workflow replacement potential.

## **Open Source vs. Closed Source: The Defining Strategic Battle**

Perhaps no question has shaped AI strategy more than: should model weights be public? This is both a technical, business, and ethical debate that defines company strategy.

| THE CASE FOR OPEN SOURCE Accelerates innovation: thousands of researchers can fine-tune and improve models. Lowers costs: no API fees for inference. Enables privacy: run locally, no data sent to cloud. Prevents monopoly: no single company can control AI. Historical parallel: Linux vs Windows — open source won long-term. Key advocates: Meta (Llama), Mistral, Hugging Face, EleutherAI, DeepSeek. |
| :---- |

| THE CASE FOR CLOSED SOURCE Safety: prevent misuse of powerful models by bad actors. Revenue: API access funds continued research. Quality: closed models currently lead on frontier benchmarks. Accountability: company responsible for model behavior. Key advocates: OpenAI, Anthropic (partially). Counter-argument: open source models have been used responsibly far more than harmfully. |
| :---- |

## **Key Patterns Since 2020**

### **Pattern 1: Scaling Works — Until It Doesn't**

From 2020-2023, the dominant strategy was 'bigger model \= better model.' GPT-3 (175B) to GPT-4, PaLM, Llama — scale was king. Then DeepSeek proved that algorithmic efficiency can dramatically offset compute costs. OpenAI's reasoning models (o1, o3) showed a new dimension: inference-time compute. The new question is no longer just 'how big is your model' but 'how efficiently can you use compute at training AND inference.'

### **Pattern 2: Safety vs. Speed Tension Never Resolves**

The OpenAI crisis of 2023 was fundamentally about this. Ilya Sutskever's departure and SSI founding. Anthropic's entire raison d'etre. EU's AI Act. Biden's Executive Order. Every breakthrough accelerates the tension: more capable \= more dangerous \= more pressure to slow down \= competitive disadvantage. No resolution in sight.

### **Pattern 3: Open Source Always Catches Up**

Every closed model eventually has an open-source equivalent. GPT-3 → Llama. DALL-E → Stable Diffusion. GPT-4 → Llama 3.1. DeepSeek V3 at $5.6M training cost. OpenAI's own GPT-OSS. The moat of closed weights is shrinking. The advantage of closed labs is now mostly in leading the frontier by 6-18 months, not permanent capability dominance.

### **Pattern 4: China Is Not Behind**

The Western narrative of 'China is catching up' was always understated. DeepSeek's January 2025 moment proved Chinese AI labs can match frontier US models on key benchmarks. The chip export controls may slow training scale but Chinese engineers are innovating around them. The AI race is genuinely global.

### **Pattern 5: The Jobs Impact Is Real and Accelerating**

From 2020-2023, AI job displacement was theoretical. From 2024-2026, it's statistical. Customer service down 80K jobs. Entry-level white-collar contracting. IBM replacing HR functions with agents. The paradox: the companies spending the most on AI infrastructure are simultaneously the ones laying off the most workers.

### **Pattern 6: Every Sector Gets Its 'ChatGPT Moment'**

Legal has Harvey. Medicine has multiple AI diagnostics. Education has AI tutors. Code has Copilot, Cursor, Claude Code. Finance has AI quantitative tools. Each vertical sees an 'oh shit' moment where AI crosses a threshold from novelty to genuinely better than the human alternative for specific tasks. These moments are accelerating in frequency.
